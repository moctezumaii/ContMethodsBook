1 Peer Review Rubric: Methods Paper Analysis

SNR 690 — Contemporary Approaches to Quantitative Methods
Reviewer name: ___________________________________
Presenter/Author: ___________________________________
Paper: Gleim et al. (2014) — Tick Phenology & Prescribed Burning

1.1 Instructions

Score each criterion on a 1–4 scale using the descriptors below. Provide specific written comments for each criterion. The goal is constructive, specific feedback — not a general rating.

1.2 Criterion 1: Methods Validity

Does the presenter/author correctly describe and justify the statistical methods used in the paper?

Score Descriptor
4 — Exemplary Accurately identifies the GEE model specification (family, link, correlation structure), explains why each choice is appropriate for the data structure, and identifies at least one defensible alternative. Correctly distinguishes GEE from GLMM.
3 — Proficient Accurately identifies most of the model specification and provides a reasonable justification for the main choices. Minor omissions (e.g., does not mention AR(1) alternative).
2 — Developing Identifies the general method (GEE) but misidentifies or omits key components (e.g., does not mention negative binomial, or confuses clusters with individual observations).
1 — Beginning Does not correctly identify the method or its justification; confuses GEE with other methods (e.g., ANOVA, standard GLM, GLMM).

1.3 Criterion 2: Interpretation

Does the presenter/author correctly interpret the model results in the context of the research question?

Score Descriptor
4 — Exemplary Correctly interprets coefficients on the log scale (IRR = exp(β)); connects results to the biological question (“prescribed burning reduces tick abundance by X% on average”); acknowledges uncertainty (CIs, robust SEs). Distinguishes marginal from conditional effects if applicable.
3 — Proficient Correctly interprets direction of effects and connects to the research question. May not fully articulate the multiplicative interpretation (exp(β)) or the role of robust SEs.
2 — Developing States whether effects are “significant” but does not quantify the magnitude or connect to the biological question. May confuse log-scale and original-scale interpretation.
1 — Beginning Does not interpret results, or interprets them incorrectly (e.g., treats log-scale coefficients as linear, or confuses p-values with effect sizes).

Score (circle):   1   2   3   4

Written comments:

 
 
 

1.4 Criterion 3: Reproducibility

Does the presenter/author provide sufficient information for a reader/classmate to reproduce or critically evaluate the analysis?

Score Descriptor
4 — Exemplary Provides (or explicitly acknowledges the paper’s lack of) a formal model equation; lists all predictors; mentions software and package; notes that raw data are not publicly available and proposes a simulation strategy. Code (if provided) runs without error.
3 — Proficient Lists main predictors and software; acknowledges data availability. Code (if provided) runs with minor modifications. Does not explicitly note the absence of a formal equation in the paper.
2 — Developing Mentions the method and software but does not specify predictors, correlation structure, or data availability. Code (if provided) has errors or does not reflect the model discussed.
1 — Beginning Provides insufficient information for a reader to understand what was done. No model specification, no code, or code is unrelated to the analysis described.

Score (circle):   1   2   3   4
